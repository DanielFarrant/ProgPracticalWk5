Uses of Computer Science in Medicine

There are many ways that Computer Science is used in Medicine. These include:

Magnetic Resonance Imaging

This is an imaging technique that is used to produce 3D images that can be examined by a doctor in order to make a diagnosis on a patient. Computers are used to piece together the thousands of scan images and create a 3D image of electrical activity in that region. A computer is also used to control the MRI scanner during the scan. The scan works by using powerful magnets to align all of the protons in your body. Short bursts of radio waves are then used to knock these out of alignment. Once the burst is over, the protons realign, emitting a radio signal that is picked up by the receiver. These signals are then used to create the MRI image.

Computer Networking

This allows patient records to be shared much more quickly than previously possible, which allows patients to be treated more quickly, especially when they are not near their local hospital. It also allows doctors to consult more easily with doctors in other hospitals which can help in the diagnosis of rare conditions,

Spectroscopy

The spectrum from the spectroscopy test is tested against a database of known substances, helping doctors to determine a substance more quickly, so they can treat the patient as soon as possible

Research

Computers are used to run medical simulations in order to find cures to diseases such as AIDS and Alzheimer's. They can also be used to sequence genomes, which are hundreds of gigabytes in size. In the future this technique may be used to guide how different patients are treated, as it will give doctors a better understanding as to what works for each different patient.
