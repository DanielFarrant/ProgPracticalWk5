Uses of Computer Science in Astronomy

There are many ways that Computer Science has aided the field of Astronomy. These include:

Automated telescope mounts

Due to software and computer hardware advancements its now possible even for amateur astronomers to more easily view objects such as stars, planets, galaxies and other various astronomical objects through the use of telescope mounts that are able to track any object a user inputs.

Detecting cosmic events using machine learning

Due to advancements in astronomical telescopes and detection methods its become increasingly harder to sift through exponentially growing amounts of data collected from the night sky, computer science can help solve this by teaching neural networks to sift through data in order to detect certain cosmic events and notify researches of any interesting findings to follow up on.

Running simulations

Incremental increases in computing power have led to the ability to run large scale n-body simulations of the solar system, galactic collisions, galaxy super clusters, etc. This allows us to predict events in advance, such as early detection of Near Earth Objects (NEOs) coming close to the Earth.
